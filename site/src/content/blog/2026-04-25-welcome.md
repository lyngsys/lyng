This is the first post on the new Lyng blog. We've been building two
things in parallel for a while now — a bytecode JavaScript engine and a
WHATWG-style HTML parser, both written in Rust — and we want a place to
publish design notes, progress reports, and the occasional war story as
we go.

Expect posts on:

- **Engine internals.** How Lyng JS lowers ECMA-262 to bytecode, how its
  garbage collector works, and how we test against Test262.
- **Parser internals.** How the HTML parser navigates the WHATWG state
  machine, how the arena-backed DOM is laid out, and how we shake out
  edge cases with html5lib.
- **Workflow and tooling.** What it's like to develop a browser-shaped
  project as a small team in the open.

You can subscribe via [RSS](/rss.xml) or watch the
[GitHub repository](https://github.com/lyngsys/lyng) for updates.
